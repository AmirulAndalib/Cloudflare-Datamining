<h4><strong>Systems Engineer - Compute Hardware</strong></h4>
<p>Cloudflare’s Infrastructure group is responsible for building our global network. Our Hardware Engineering team helps research, design, test, and deploy new equipment enabling compute, storage, and network. Deployed across 200+ cities in 100+ countries, the hardware we select helps improve the security, reliability, and performance of the Internet.</p>
<h4><strong>What you'll do</strong></h4>
<p>We need to make thoughtful infrastructure choices affecting a significant portion of the Internet. Hardware we work with includes servers, routers, switches, optical equipment, power distribution units, cables, optics, and more. As a Hardware Systems Engineer, you will work with colleagues on the Hardware Engineering, Product teams, and Hardware Sourcing teams to evaluate and inform decisions around new hardware.</p>
<ul>
	<li>Design hardware (Compute, AI/ML, and accelerators across traditional system boundaries which are efficient, secure, reliable and serviceable) for cloud customer workloads</li>
	<li>Develop and deploy Cloudflare’s next generation of servers, and other innovative technologies as part of Cloudflare’s data center infrastructure&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Experiment with different designs and solutions for Cloudflare’s hardware and work cross-functionally with Product teams and Hardware Sourcing to enact your suggestions globally</li>
	<li>Work with vendors to obtain, debug, and maintain the most performant, power efficient and cost effective next-generation compute hardware and firmware for Cloudflare’s workloads</li>
	<li>Develop tools and automation to evaluate and optimize hardware performance</li>
	<li>Communicate your results and updates through blog posts, internal talks, and tickets</li>
</ul>
<h4><strong>Examples of desirable skills, knowledge and experience</strong></h4>
<ul>
	<li>Extensive experience with computer hardware architecture, design engineering, validation, performance benchmarking, debugging, and deployment used in the data center</li>
	<li>Relevant engineering disciplines include ASIC development, Computer hardware engineering and Electrical engineering.</li>
	<li>Previous experience in testing, measuring, and/or simulating hardware performance and an understanding of no-stone-left-unturned experimentation</li>
	<li>Track record of driving engineering projects from idea to experiment to implementation</li>
	<li>Demonstrated success in working with ODMs and technology vendors</li>
	<li>Professional-level proficiency in Python, Go, or similar programming and/or scripting languages</li>
	<li>Good Linux systems knowledge</li>
</ul>
